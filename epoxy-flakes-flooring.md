What Are Epoxy Flakes and How Do They Work?
Epoxy flake flooring is created by broadcasting small vinyl or acrylic chips , called flakes or chips , into a freshly applied wet epoxy base coat. The chips stick to the surface and are then sealed under one or two clear epoxy or polyurethane topcoats. The finished floor has a decorative, slightly textured appearance that resembles terrazzo or speckled stone.
The vinyl flakes serve several purposes beyond aesthetics. They add a degree of texture to the surface, which increases grip underfoot. They also hide minor surface imperfections in the concrete below and add an extra layer of depth and dimension to the finished floor that solid,color epoxy simply cannot achieve.
Types of Epoxy Flake Flooring Systems
Standard Broadcast Flake System: Flakes are tossed uniformly onto the wet base coat until a consistent coverage is achieved. The topcoat seals everything flat. Best for garages, utility rooms, and workshops.
Full,Broadcast (Solid Flake) System: Flakes are applied heavily and completely cover the base coat, creating a dense, almost mosaic,like surface. The topcoat fills the gaps between chips. More decorative and great for showrooms and commercial lobbies.
Partial,Broadcast Flake System: Fewer flakes are used, allowing the base coat color to show through. Creates a more subtle, artistic effect with visible contrast between the base and the chips.
Colored Base with Contrasting Flakes: Matching or contrasting colored base coats are used alongside specific flake blends to create custom color stories. Ideal for branded commercial spaces.
Anti,Slip Flake System: Aluminum oxide grit is added to the topcoat over the flake system for areas requiring enhanced traction, such as kitchen floors, ramps, and pool surrounds.

Epoxy Flake Flooring Installation Process
Surface Preparation: Diamond grinding or shot blasting to achieve the correct concrete surface profile. Cracks, joints, and divots are repaired with epoxy mortar.
Moisture and pH Testing: We test concrete moisture levels before priming. Excessive moisture can cause adhesion failure. If required, a moisture,tolerant primer is used.
Primer Application: A penetrating epoxy primer is applied to seal the concrete and ensure the base coat bonds firmly.
Base Coat Application: The colored epoxy base coat is applied evenly across the floor. Timing here is critical , the flakes must be broadcast while the coat is still wet.
Flake Broadcasting: Flakes are scattered over the wet surface either by hand or with a broadcast spreader. For full,broadcast systems, excess flakes are removed after curing with a rigid blade or floor grinder.
Grout Coat (Full Broadcast Only): A thin epoxy grout coat is applied over the cured full,broadcast surface to fill gaps between chips and create a flat surface before the topcoat.
Clear Topcoat: One or two coats of a UV,stable polyurethane topcoat seal and protect the flake layer, providing the final gloss level and long,term durability.
Curing and Handover: Light foot traffic is possible within 24 hours. Full cure in 5 to 7 days. We walk you through care and maintenance at handover.

Frequently Asked Questions About Epoxy Flakes
Can I choose my own flake color combination?
Yes. We carry a large selection of standard flake blends and can also custom,mix colors to match your design specification. Bring a color swatch, mood board, or Pantone reference to our Al Quoz showroom and our team will work with you to create the perfect blend.
How long does a flake epoxy floor last?
With proper maintenance, a quality epoxy flake floor installed by our team will typically last 10 to 15 years in a residential garage. In commercial settings with heavier traffic, plan for 5 to 10 years before a maintenance topcoat is needed to refresh the surface.
Is the flake surface rough or smooth?
A partial,broadcast flake floor with a standard topcoat feels smooth to the touch with very slight texture. A full,broadcast system without anti,slip grit feels similar to a fine sandpaper texture, which provides natural grip. If you want more traction, anti,slip grit is added to the topcoat.
Can epoxy flakes be applied in a bathroom?
Yes, with the right topcoat and anti,slip specification. The key is ensuring the floor is properly waterproofed at the perimeter and that the topcoat has adequate slip resistance. We handle all of this as part of the installation process.
