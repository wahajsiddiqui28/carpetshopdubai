What Is Metallic Epoxy Flooring?
Metallic epoxy flooring is a specialized epoxy coating system that incorporates metallic oxide or mica,based pigment powders into a clear or pigmented epoxy base coat. When applied, these pigments create unpredictable, flowing patterns as the resin spreads and the pigments disperse. The effect is further enhanced by the applicator who uses tools , squeegees, notched trowels, and air movement , to guide the pigments into specific patterns.
The finished surface has extraordinary depth. Because the metallic pigments are suspended within the body of the epoxy rather than sitting on the surface, the floor appears to have dimension and movement when viewed from different angles. As lighting changes throughout the day, the floor shifts and shimmers in ways that make it feel alive.
This complexity is also what makes professional installation absolutely critical. A metallic epoxy floor applied without skill and experience will look muddied, blotchy, or inconsistent. Our trained applicators understand how to control the pigment movement and achieve consistently stunning results across large floor areas.
Types and Styles of Metallic Epoxy Flooring
Pearl and Shimmer Effect: Light, subtle metallic sheen using pearl or silver pigments. Creates an elegant, sophisticated look suited to bedrooms, bathrooms, and high,end retail spaces.
Ocean and River Effect: Deep blue, teal, and aqua pigments are blended to mimic the appearance of flowing water or underwater coral. Spectacular for pool areas, spa spaces, and creative studio environments.
Lava and Fire Effect: Red, orange, and gold pigments swirl and contrast to create the visual energy of flowing lava. High,impact and dramatic; ideal for lobbies, bar areas, and entertainment spaces.
Galaxy and Cosmos Effect: Dark base coats with silver, violet, and iridescent pigments create the illusion of looking into deep space. A bold statement floor for restaurants, clubs, and premium residences.
Marble and Stone Effect: White, grey, and black pigments are carefully guided to mimic the veining of natural marble. Achieves the look of expensive stone at a fraction of the cost and with far greater durability.
Gold Leaf and Bronze Effect: Rich gold, bronze, and copper pigments create an opulent, high,status finish. Particularly popular in hotel lobbies, mosque interiors, and luxury retail.
Monochrome Metallic: A single metallic pigment in silver, gunmetal, champagne, or charcoal creates a clean, ultra,modern floor for minimalist interiors and showrooms.

Our Metallic Epoxy Flooring Installation Process
Design Consultation: We discuss color preferences, pattern direction, and the mood you want to create. Our team can show you portfolio images and physical samples of each metallic pigment during the free site visit.
Substrate Assessment and Preparation: The concrete must be clean, dry, and mechanically prepared. Diamond grinding achieves the required surface profile. Cracks and voids are repaired. Moisture levels are tested and, if high, a moisture,tolerant primer is used.
Primer Coat: A clear epoxy primer is roller,applied to seal the substrate and create the chemical bond required for the metallic coat above.
Metallic Base Coat Application: The metallic pigment is blended into the clear epoxy and applied across the primed surface. The applicator then immediately works with squeegees, spiked rollers, and compressed air to move the pigments and create the desired pattern effect.
Second Metallic Layer (If Required): For more complex multi,tone effects, a second metallic layer using a contrasting or complementary pigment is applied while the first is still tacky, allowing the two to blend organically.
UV,Stable Clear Topcoat: One or two coats of a UV,stable aliphatic polyurethane topcoat are applied to seal and protect the metallic layer. This prevents yellowing and maintains the brilliant gloss finish for years.
Curing and Handover: The floor is left to cure in a dust,free environment. Light traffic is safe after 24 hours; full cure takes 5 to 7 days. We provide a detailed maintenance guide at handover.

Frequently Asked Questions About Metallic Epoxy Flooring
Can I choose the exact pattern for my metallic epoxy floor?
You can select the color palette, the dominant pigments, and the general direction or style of the pattern. However, because the metallic pigments move freely in the wet resin, the final pattern is influenced by the applicator's technique and can never be precisely predetermined. This organic quality is exactly what makes each installation unique and genuinely artistic.
Will the metallic floor look the same in all lighting conditions?
No, and that is one of its most appealing qualities. A metallic epoxy floor changes dramatically as the direction and color temperature of light changes. Under warm downlights, gold and bronze pigments glow richly. Under natural daylight, silver and pearl pigments sparkle and shift. The floor is genuinely different at different times of day, which makes the space feel alive and dynamic.
Is metallic epoxy flooring suitable for outdoor areas?
Standard metallic epoxy is formulated for indoor use. For covered outdoor areas like entrance canopies, covered terraces, or shaded car ports, we use an aliphatic metallic system specifically designed to resist UV degradation. For fully exposed outdoor areas, we would recommend a different system entirely. Our team will advise the best approach during the consultation.
How do I clean and maintain a metallic epoxy floor?
Daily dry mopping removes dust and grit. Weekly damp mopping with a pH,neutral cleaner maintains the gloss. Avoid abrasive scrubbing pads, bleach, and acidic cleaners. Place felt pads under furniture legs to prevent scratching. For high,traffic areas, a maintenance topcoat every 5 to 7 years will restore full gloss.
